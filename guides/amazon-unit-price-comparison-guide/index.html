Unit price comparison: make Amazon pack sizes comparable

Listings sell packages. You buy units. Until you normalize, the “best deal” badge is just layout.

Unit price = Pack price / Quantity in a common unit

FamilyNormalize toWatch-out
Weightoz or lbNet weight vs shipping weight
Volumefl oz or mLConcentrates vs ready-to-use
Countper piece“Up to” sheet counts

Worked example

Oz trap. A $12 / 10 oz jar is $1.20/oz. A $18 / 16 oz jar is $1.125/oz. The larger jar wins on unit price even when the headline price is higher.

Mixed units need a family first

Convert lb→oz or L→mL inside one family. Do not compare oz to count. Good comparators refuse illegal mixes instead of inventing fake math.

When unit price is not enough

Durables need cost-per-use. Unit price wins for consumables; lifetime cost wins for shoes, tools, and appliances.

Checklist

  • Same product family compared
  • Quantities converted to one unit
  • Unit prices ranked
  • Quality/return risk considered
  • Subscribe &amp; Save math checked separately

FAQ

Why doesn’t Amazon always show unit price?

Display rules vary by category and seller data quality. Assume you may need to compute it yourself.

Do coupons change the method?

Use the price you actually pay after coupon, then divide by quantity.

What about shipping?

If one option adds shipping, include it in pack price before dividing — otherwise “free” shipping offers distort the winner.
