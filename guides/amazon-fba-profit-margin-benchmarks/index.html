What is a good FBA profit margin before ads?

Margin without an ad ceiling is incomplete. Still, you need a pre-ad floor — or every campaign debate starts from fantasy.

Pre-ad margin % = (Sell − cost − referral − FBA − fuel) / Sell × 100

Pre-ad marginReadingNext step
Under 15%Fragile for most PPC-dependent launchesReprice, lighten package, or pass
15–30%Workable if ACoS is controlledSet breakeven ACoS; stress-test payback
30%+Strong cushionStill model ads and inventory cash

Worked example

Thin margin trap. 18% pre-ad margin with a 15% TACoS leaves ~3% — technically positive, operationally fragile after returns and anomalies.

Benchmarks are not universal laws

Consumables with high repeat and low returns can survive thinner margins than fashion with size returns. Use bands as tripwires, then adjust for category reality — not influencer screenshots.

Always pair margin with cash recovery

A 35% margin SKU that needs 14 months to sell through can still wreck cash flow. Run units-to-breakeven on the real PO size before you celebrate percentage margin.

Checklist

  • Pre-ad margin calculated in dollars and %
  • Breakeven ACoS known
  • Target ACoS set below breakeven
  • PO payback units estimated
  • Return rate sanity check for category

FAQ

Should margin include my salary?

For sourcing go/no-go, contribution after Amazon fees and COGS is the first filter. Owner salary belongs in a fuller P&amp;L once the SKU is real.

Is ROI better than margin?

Use both. Margin shows unit health; ROI on inventory shows cash efficiency across the PO.

What if my comps sell at a price that kills margin?

That is your answer: pass, change the offer, or find a structural cost advantage — do not invent a fantasy ASP.
