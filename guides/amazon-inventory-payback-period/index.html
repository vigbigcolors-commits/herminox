Worked example

Velocity matter. 2,400 units to recover at 400 units/month ≈ 6 months. Same PO at 200 units/month ≈ 12 months — a different business risk.

Seasonality lies to annual averages

A Q4 spike can hide a dead Q2. Model payback on conservative velocity, not peak week screenshots from Keepa.

Ads can pull payback forward — or destroy contribution

Paid velocity shortens payback only if contribution after ads stays sensible. Fast sell-through at negative contribution is just a quicker way to lose the PO.

Checklist

  • Units-to-recover known
  • Conservative monthly velocity set
  • Payback months computed
  • Compared to storage/aging risk
  • Reorder timing planned after payback

FAQ

What payback is “good”?

Depends on capital cost and category. Many operators dislike double-digit months on unproven SKUs — treat long payback as higher risk, not automatically “patient investing.”

How do stockouts affect payback?

Stockouts pause recovery and can erase rank. Payback math assumes you can actually sell; inbound planning is part of the model.

Is sell-through the same as sessions?

No. Sell-through is units sold against inventory. Sessions without conversion do not recover cash.
