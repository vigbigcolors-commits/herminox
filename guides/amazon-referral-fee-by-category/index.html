Amazon referral fees by category: flat vs tiered

A flat “15%” mental model is wrong for whole categories — and expensive when it is wrong on the high side or the low side.

Worked example

Apparel tier sketch. On a tiered Apparel schedule, a $14.99 shirt may sit mostly in a lower band, while a $22 shirt pays a lower rate on the first slice and a higher rate above it. A flat 17% on $14.99 overstates the fee; a flat 5% on $40 understates it.

Category placement is a margin decision

Two valid browse paths can imply different referral schedules. Before you source, check which category your comparable ASINs actually sit in — and whether that category is flat or tiered. A few points of referral on every unit compounds faster than most packaging tweaks.

Minimum fees punish cheap SKUs

On low sell prices, the minimum referral can dominate. A $3 accessory at a “low %” category can still lose money after minimum referral + fulfillment. Always run dollar fees, not only percentages.

Checklist

  • Identify likely category from live comps
  • Check whether that category is tiered
  • Compute referral in dollars
  • Compare against $0.30 minimum
  • Re-check after any price change

FAQ

Where do I get official referral rates?

Amazon publishes selling-fee schedules in Seller Central help documentation. Schedules change — verify the date on the page you use.

Can I pick a category just for lower fees?

Mis-categorization risks suppression and account issues. Model honest placement, not fee shopping.

Do referral fees apply to FBM the same way?

Referral applies to sales regardless of FBA vs FBM; fulfillment differs. Always separate the two in your model.
