Launch ACoS vs profit ACoS: two modes, one honest ledger

Launch mode is allowed. Infinite launch mode is how brands rent page-one forever and call it growth.

Worked example

Switch rule. If after 4–6 weeks organic share is flat and TACoS is not improving, you are not investing — you are stuck. Cut to profit ACoS or fix the offer.

Write the launch hypothesis down

Example: “Spend up to $X over 30 days to reach Y units/day and Z% organic.” If you cannot write it, you cannot audit it. Pair with inventory so you do not stockout mid-push or overbuy into a failed test.

Inventory coupling

Aggressive launch ACoS on a thin inbound PO creates stockouts that erase rank gains. Aggressive inventory without an ad plan creates aged stock. Launch is a cash + ads + inbound triangle.

Checklist

  • Breakeven ACoS known
  • Launch budget and end date set
  • Inventory covers the test
  • Weekly TACoS/organic review
  • Switch criteria defined

FAQ

How long should launch mode last?

Often weeks, not quarters. Category competition changes the calendar — the point is a pre-committed end, not a feeling.

Can launch ACoS exceed breakeven?

Yes, as capped CAC. Never as an unlabeled default.

What metric proves launch worked?

Improving organic share and falling TACoS at stable or rising sales — not ACoS alone.
