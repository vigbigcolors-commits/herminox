ACoS vs TACoS: stop using one metric for two jobs

ACoS tells you about ad efficiency. TACoS tells you about ad burden on the whole business. Confusing them creates false confidence.

TACoS ≈ ACoS × (1 − organic share)

MetricDenominatorUse it to
ACoSAd-attributed salesJudge campaign/keyword efficiency
TACoSTotal salesJudge ad load on the SKU/account
Breakeven ACoSMargin-based ceilingSet maximum affordable ACoS

Worked example

Organic flywheel. ACoS 30% with 10% organic → TACoS ~27%. Same ACoS with 60% organic → TACoS ~12%. The ad engine did not magically improve — the denominator did.

Healthy pattern as listings mature

Early: higher ACoS, higher TACoS, low organic. Mature: ACoS may stay moderate while TACoS falls if organic share grows. If both stay high for months, you are renting sales, not building an asset.

Reporting tip

Review ACoS weekly for optimization. Review TACoS weekly for strategy. Never celebrate a TACoS drop that came only from pausing ads and watching sales collapse.

Checklist

  • Track ACoS and TACoS on the same dashboard
  • Estimate organic share monthly
  • Compare ACoS to breakeven, not to internet averages
  • Separate launch budgets from evergreen budgets

FAQ

What is a good TACoS?

It depends on margin and growth stage. Use breakeven ACoS and contribution after ads — not a universal TACoS myth number.

Can TACoS be higher than ACoS?

No — if definitions are standard, TACoS ≤ ACoS because total sales ≥ ad sales.

Should beginners ignore TACoS?

No. Beginners over-focus on ACoS while burning cash. TACoS keeps the whole P&amp;L in view.
