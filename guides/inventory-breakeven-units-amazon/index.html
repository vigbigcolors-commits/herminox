Percentage margin can look great while cash stays trapped. Units-to-recover is the question factories understand.

Worked example

PO sketch. PO cash $8,000. Contribution after Amazon fees $4/unit. You need ~2,000 units sold to recover cash — before counting profit beyond breakeven.

Define “cash outlay” honestly

Include product, inbound freight, prep, and other cash that leaves before sell-through. Excluding inbound is a classic way to fake a fast payback.

Fee truth required

Contribution per unit must use real referral + FBA + fuel. Inflated contribution understates units-to-recover and overstates how safe the PO is.

Checklist

  • Full PO cash listed
  • Contribution/unit after fees
  • Units-to-recover computed
  • Sell-through timeline estimated
  • Storage risk considered for slow SKUs

FAQ

Is breakeven units the same as MOQ?

No. MOQ is what the supplier forces; breakeven units is what economics require. Sometimes MOQ &gt; sane breakeven — that is a pass signal.

Do returns change the number?

Yes. Effective contribution falls when return rates rise. Haircut contribution for high-return categories.

Should I include ad spend in contribution?

For cash recovery of inventory, start with fee-adjusted product contribution, then layer ads as a separate cash drain with its own budget.
