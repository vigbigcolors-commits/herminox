Tiered referral fees: why Apparel and Jewelry break flat-rate math

Seven categories do not use a single percentage on the whole price. If your spreadsheet assumes flat rates there, your margin is fiction.

Worked example

Band thinking. Suppose a schedule charges a lower % on the first $15 and a higher % above. A $14 item and a $30 item are not “the same category rate.” Price changes move you across bands nonlinearly.

Price tests change fee % in tiered categories

In a flat category, a $1 price cut changes referral roughly linearly. In a tiered category, a $1 cut can also change which band dollars sit in — so contribution can jump more than you expect. Always re-run fee math when you A/B price.

PPC ceilings depend on correct referral

Breakeven ACoS is margin-based. If referral is wrong by 3–5 points, your entire PPC “safe zone” shifts. Fix fee truth before you scale Sponsored Products.

Checklist

  • Confirm category is tiered
  • Use band math, not a single %
  • Recompute after price changes
  • Feed correct referral into ACoS breakeven

FAQ

Which categories are tiered?

Commonly cited tiered groups include Apparel, Baby, Furniture, Grocery &amp; Gourmet, Jewelry, Shoes, and Watches — verify against Amazon’s current US fee schedule.

Can I approximate with an average %?

For rough brainstorming only. For POs and ads, use band math or a calculator that implements bands.

Does tiering affect FBA fulfillment?

Tiered referral is separate from fulfillment size tiers. Model both; do not conflate them.
