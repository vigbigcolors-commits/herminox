The FBA fuel surcharge: 3.5% that shows up after you thought fees were done

Fuel is not a new referral rate. It is a percentage of the fulfillment fee — easy to miss in old spreadsheets built before April 2026.

Worked example

Volume math. At $4.60 fulfillment, fuel ≈ $0.16/unit. On 2,000 units that is ~$320 — often more than a packaging redesign you debated for weeks.

Where people mis-model it

Do not apply 3.5% to the sell price. Do not apply it to referral. It rides on fulfillment. Turning it “off” in a calculator to make a product look green is how you invent phantom margin.

How it interacts with inventory payback

Fuel raises cost per unit sold, which raises units-to-breakeven on a fixed purchase order. If your payback model ignores fuel, your cash-recovery date is late by design.

Checklist

  • Fulfillment fee estimated from size/weight
  • Fuel toggled on
  • Per-unit and per-PO totals reviewed
  • Margin re-checked after fuel

FAQ

Is the fuel surcharge permanent?

Amazon can change surcharges. Treat 3.5% as the active published figure until Amazon updates documentation — then update your model the same week.

Does fuel apply to FBM?

This surcharge is an FBA fulfillment concept. FBM has different shipping cost realities you model yourself.

Should I raise price to cover fuel?

Sometimes. First see whether size-tier optimization or landed-cost negotiation is cheaper than a price hike that hurts conversion.
