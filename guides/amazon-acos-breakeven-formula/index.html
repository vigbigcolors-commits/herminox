Breakeven ACoS: the formula that stops hope-based PPC

If you do not know the ACoS where ad sales stop being profitable, you are not “testing” — you are donating margin.

Breakeven ACoS % ≈ Pre-ad profit per unit / Sell price × 100

Pre-ad profitSell priceBreakeven ACoS
$6$2425%
$9$3030%
$12$4030%

Worked example

Numbers. Sell $30, pre-ad profit $9 → breakeven ACoS = 30%. A 35% ACoS on ad-attributed sales loses money on those sales even if total store revenue looks fine.

Breakeven is a ceiling, not a target

Operators usually aim for 50–75% of breakeven ACoS so returns, fee drift, and bad weeks do not erase contribution. Launch periods can temporarily run hotter — with a budget cap and an end date.

Garbage in: fees wrong → ACoS wrong

Compute pre-ad profit with real referral and FBA (including fuel). Then compute breakeven ACoS. Skipping the fee step produces a confident, incorrect bid strategy.

Checklist

  • Pre-ad profit known in dollars
  • Breakeven ACoS computed
  • Target ACoS set below breakeven
  • Campaign budgets capped

FAQ

Is breakeven ACoS the same as TACoS?

No. ACoS uses ad-attributed sales; TACoS uses total sales. Both matter; they answer different questions.

Does breakeven include organic sales?

Classic breakeven ACoS is about ad-driven sales profitability. Organic mix shows up when you interpret TACoS.

What if my ACoS is above breakeven but rank is rising?

That can be a deliberate launch investment. Treat it as capped CAC, not as “good ACoS.”
