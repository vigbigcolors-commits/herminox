Capital tied up: the FBA number that margin % hides

You can be “profitable on paper” and still unable to fund the next PO because cash is sitting in plastic in a warehouse.

Worked example

Reorder discipline. If $12,000 is still tied in slow colorways, a new “winning” SKU PO may need outside cash. That is an inventory portfolio problem, not a motivation problem.

Variants multiply trapped cash

Size/color expansion feels like merchandising and behaves like mini-POs. Track tied capital by variant, not only by parent ASIN averages.

Tie capital reviews to fee models

When contribution/unit shrinks (fee hikes, price war), the same unit count represents worse capital efficiency. Re-run FBA economics when the catalog gets heavy.

Checklist

  • Landed cost per unit known
  • Units on hand by SKU
  • Tied capital summed
  • Dead stock flagged
  • Next PO sized against free cash

FAQ

Is tied capital the same as COGS on the P&amp;L?

Related but not identical. Tied capital is a cash/balance-sheet view of unsold inventory; P&amp;L recognizes COGS when sold.

Should I liquidate to free capital?

Sometimes yes — paying to exit can beat months of storage and opportunity cost. Run numbers; do not emotional-hoard.

How often should I review tied capital?

Weekly for paid launches; at least monthly for the full catalog.
