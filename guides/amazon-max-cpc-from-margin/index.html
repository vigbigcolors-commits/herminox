Max CPC: turn margin into a bid ceiling

Bids without a max CPC are vibes. Conversion rate and breakeven ACoS give you a number you can defend.

Worked example

Worked bid. $30 price, 40% breakeven ACoS, 10% CVR → max CPC = 30 × 0.40 × 0.10 = $1.20. Many operators bid 50–80% of max CPC for cushion.

CVR is the hidden lever

Improving the listing (images, title, reviews velocity quality) raises CVR and raises affordable CPC without raising ACoS targets. Fixing conversion often beats blindly raising bids.

Placement modifiers need the same ceiling logic

Top-of-search multipliers can push effective CPC above your max. Recalculate after modifiers — not only on base bid.

Checklist

  • Breakeven ACoS from real fees
  • CVR from recent Sponsored Products data
  • Max CPC computed
  • Bid set below max
  • Modifiers checked

FAQ

Which CVR should I use?

Prefer ad CVR for the campaign type you are bidding on. Blended store CVR can mislead on cold keywords.

What if max CPC is below $0.40?

That SKU may not support paid traffic at current margin/CVR. Improve offer economics before buying clicks.

Does this work for Sponsored Brands?

Same economics apply, but attribution and CVR differ — recompute with SB-specific conversion assumptions.
