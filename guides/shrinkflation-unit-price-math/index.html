Shrinkflation: catch a silent price hike with unit math

When the bag looks familiar but net weight fell 8%, you got a price increase without a red clearance tag.

Effective price change % ≈ New unit price / Old unit price − 1

Worked example

Sheets example. Old: $20 / 200 count = $0.10 each. New: $20 / 180 count = $0.111. That is an ~11% unit-price hike at the same sticker.

How to audit a “same” product

Save net quantity from the previous order email or label photo. Compare to today’s detail page. Marketing copy will not announce shrinkflation; the nutrition/net-content line will.

Multipacks can hide it twice

Fewer units per inner pack × fewer inners per case is a double shrink. Always expand to total count/weight before dividing.

Checklist

  • Old net qty recorded
  • New net qty recorded
  • Unit prices compared
  • Decide switch brand / size / skip

FAQ

Is shrinkflation illegal?

Generally it is a legal packaging/pricing practice when net contents are disclosed. Your defense is measurement, not outrage alone.

Does Subscribe &amp; Save protect me?

No. Discounts apply to the current pack. If quantity shrank, unit price can still worsen.

What if quantity is missing on the page?

Open images/label text or ask the seller. Without quantity, refuse the comparison — do not guess.
